About me

I'm Doktor! I'm a photographer living in Boston, Massachusetts. In my free time, I photograph big animals.

Contact info and other links can be found on my other website.
You can find my photos on this website and on Twitter.
I keep track of my work on my Trello board, which I update frequently.

Photography

I started taking photos in September 2015, my senior year of high school. Throughout the year, I took event photos for the Yearbook Club. In January 2016, I attended my first convention, Anthro New England 2016, where I volunteered in the Photo Room. As the saying goes, the rest is history.

I'm self-taught — I've never had any formal training in photography. There's a list of resources I refer to at the bottom of this page. The most important thing you can do is take lots of photos!

I started with my parents' Canon EOS 550D. I upgraded to a Canon EOS 80D in June 2016. I currently shoot with a Canon EOS 5D Mark IV, as of October 2017. The full list of equipment that I use is below.

I'm the head photographer for Anthro New England, a furry convention held annually in Massachusetts. I photograph a few main-stage events, organize and run the Photo Room, and take the Fursuit Group Photo. I first volunteered in the Photo Room in 2016, and joined the staff to establish the Photography Department in 2017.

Commissions

I currently only accept commissions privately from friends and through friend referrals.
